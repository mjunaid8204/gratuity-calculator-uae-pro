How to Calculate Gratuity for Unlimited Contract in UAE (2024 Step-by-Step Guide)

📅 June 10, 2024🕑 6 min read📖 9,200 views

Understanding how to calculate gratuity for an unlimited contract in the UAE depends on whether you resigned or were terminated, and how many years you have served. This guide walks you through every scenario with real numerical examples.

What is an Unlimited Contract in UAE?

An unlimited (open-ended) contract in the UAE has no fixed end date. It continues until one party decides to terminate it with proper notice. Under Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021, unlimited contracts must specify a probation period of no more than 6 months.

Unlimited contracts are the most common type of employment agreement in the UAE private sector. They give both employer and employee flexibility, but also come with specific gratuity entitlement rules.

The Core Formula

🧮 Step 1: Calculate Daily Wage

Daily Wage = Monthly Basic Salary ÷ 30

🧮 Step 2: Calculate Gratuity Days

Based on years served and resignation or termination (see tables below)

🧮 Step 3: Multiply

Gratuity = Daily Wage × Qualifying Days × Years Served

Important: Use your basic salary only. Housing, transport, and other allowances are excluded unless your contract states otherwise.

Scenario 1: Unlimited Contract — You Resigned

When you resign from an unlimited contract, your gratuity entitlement is proportional to your years of service:

Years of ServiceGratuity RateMultiplier
Less than 1 yearNo gratuity0
1 year to 3 years1/3 of 21 days per year7 days/year
3 years to 5 years2/3 of 21 days per year14 days/year
More than 5 yearsFull 21 days per year21 days/year

Example 1A: Resigned After 2.5 Years

👥 Employee: Sarah | Basic Salary: AED 6,000 | Service: 2 years 6 months (2.5 years)

Daily WageAED 6,000 ÷ 30 = AED 200
Service Period2.5 years (falls in 1-3 years bracket)
Gratuity Rate1/3 of 21 days = 7 days per year
CalculationAED 200 × 7 days × 2.5 years
✓ Total GratuityAED 3,500

Example 1B: Resigned After 4 Years

👥 Employee: Ahmed | Basic Salary: AED 8,000 | Service: 4 years exactly

Daily WageAED 8,000 ÷ 30 = AED 266.67
Service Period4 years (falls in 3-5 years bracket)
Gratuity Rate2/3 of 21 days = 14 days per year
CalculationAED 266.67 × 14 days × 4 years
✓ Total GratuityAED 14,933

Example 1C: Resigned After 7 Years

👥 Employee: Priya | Basic Salary: AED 10,000 | Service: 7 years

Daily WageAED 10,000 ÷ 30 = AED 333.33
Service Period7 years (above 5 years = full rate)
Gratuity RateFull 21 days per year
CalculationAED 333.33 × 21 days × 7 years
✓ Total GratuityAED 49,000

Scenario 2: Unlimited Contract — Terminated by Employer

If your employer terminates your unlimited contract, you receive more generous gratuity:

Years of ServiceGratuity RateNotes
Less than 1 yearNo gratuityMinimum service not met
1 to 5 years21 days basic salary per yearFull rate from year 1
Above 5 years21 days/yr (first 5) + 30 days/yr (above 5)Higher rate for extra years
Maximum cap: 2 years total basic salary

Example 2A: Terminated After 3 Years

👥 Employee: Malik | Basic Salary: AED 7,000 | Service: 3 years | Terminated

Daily WageAED 7,000 ÷ 30 = AED 233.33
Gratuity Rate21 days per year (termination = full rate)
CalculationAED 233.33 × 21 × 3
✓ Total GratuityAED 14,700

Example 2B: Terminated After 8 Years (Over 5 Years)

👥 Employee: Fatima | Basic Salary: AED 12,000 | Service: 8 years | Terminated

Daily WageAED 12,000 ÷ 30 = AED 400
First 5 yearsAED 400 × 21 days × 5 = AED 42,000
Years 6, 7, 8 (3 extra years)AED 400 × 30 days × 3 = AED 36,000
Maximum CheckAED 12,000 × 24 months = AED 288,000 cap
✓ Total GratuityAED 78,000 (within cap)

Key Things to Remember

  • Basic salary only: Never include housing, transport, or allowances
  • Partial year counts: If you served 3 years and 6 months, use 3.5 years
  • Unpaid leave deducted: Days of unpaid leave reduce your total service period
  • 2-year cap applies: Maximum gratuity = 24 months of basic salary
  • Resignation vs Termination matters: Termination always gives full gratuity rate; resignation gives proportional rate
  • Misconduct exception: Termination for gross misconduct under Article 44 may forfeit gratuity

FAQs: Unlimited Contract Gratuity

Does it matter why I resigned from my unlimited contract?
No. Whether you resigned for personal reasons, a better job, or any other reason, your gratuity entitlement for an unlimited contract follows the same proportional rates. The reason for resignation does not affect the amount, only whether you resigned or were terminated does.
If I was offered a voluntary redundancy package, is that separate from gratuity?
Yes. Voluntary Separation / Redundancy packages offered by employers are additional compensation and do not replace your legal gratuity entitlement. You are entitled to both.
My employer told me to resign instead of terminating me. Does this affect my gratuity?
This is a common issue. If your employer pressured you to resign to avoid paying termination gratuity, you may have grounds to file a complaint with MOHRE claiming constructive dismissal. Document all communications as evidence. Call MOHRE on 800 60.
I worked for 4 years and 11 months. Do I get the 5-year rate?
No. For resignation, the 5-year full rate requires completing exactly 5 years or more. At 4 years 11 months (approximately 4.92 years), you would still fall in the 3-5 year bracket and receive 2/3 of the 21-day rate for your total service. However, the partial months still count proportionally.
