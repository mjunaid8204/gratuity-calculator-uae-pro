UAE Gratuity Law 2024: Your Complete Guide to Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021

📅 June 15, 2024 🕑 8 min read 📖 12,540 views 📌 Updated for 2024

Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021 is the most significant change to UAE employment law in decades. This guide breaks down exactly what changed, how it affects your gratuity entitlement, and what every UAE employee needs to know in 2024.

What Changed Under the New UAE Labour Law

Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021 (effective February 2, 2022) replaced Federal Law No. 8 of 1980 as the primary piece of UAE private sector employment legislation. The new law introduced several important changes that directly affect gratuity:

  • Elimination of the "Resignation Penalty": Under the old law, employees who resigned after less than 1 year received no gratuity, and those resigning in 1-3 years received only 1/3. The new law maintains this structure but introduces more employee-friendly provisions.
  • New Contract Types: The old unlimited/limited system was updated. Now there are specific rules for part-time workers, remote workers, and flexible work arrangements.
  • Probation Period Changes: The maximum probation period is now 6 months. Employees dismissed during probation have specific rights.
  • Article 51 Protections: Stronger protections against arbitrary termination and expanded reasons that qualify as "arbitrary dismissal" entitling employees to additional compensation.
📌 Key Date

The new Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021 came into effect on February 2, 2022. Gratuity calculations for service completed before this date may be subject to different rules. Consult MOHRE for detailed guidance on transitional cases.

Who Is Eligible for Gratuity in UAE

To qualify for UAE gratuity (End of Service Benefit), the following conditions must be met:

  • Minimum 1 year of continuous service with the same employer
  • Working in the UAE private sector (government employees have separate schemes)
  • Employment must have ended (resignation, termination, contract expiry, retirement, or death)
  • Not dismissed for gross misconduct under Article 44 of the Labour Law

Gratuity is NOT applicable to:

  • Employees who have completed less than 1 full year of service
  • Employees dismissed for gross misconduct (specific circumstances only)
  • Public sector (government) employees (covered by different laws)
  • Domestic workers (covered by Federal Law No. 10 of 2017)

The Official Gratuity Calculation Formula

The fundamental formula for UAE gratuity is based on the employee's basic salary only:

🧮 The Basic Formula

Daily Wage = Monthly Basic Salary ÷ 30
Gratuity = Daily Wage × Number of Qualifying Days × Years of Service

The "Number of Qualifying Days" depends on the contract type and years served:

  • 21 days per year: For the first 5 years of service (most scenarios)
  • 30 days per year: For service beyond 5 years (applicable in termination and limited contract scenarios)

⚠️ Important: Gratuity is calculated on BASIC salary only. Housing allowance, transport allowance, food allowance, and other benefits are excluded unless your employment contract specifically includes them.

When Can Gratuity Be Reduced or Withheld

Under Article 44 of Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021, an employer may dismiss an employee without gratuity only in very specific circumstances:

  • If the employee assumed a false identity or submitted forged documents
  • If the employee committed a mistake that caused substantial financial loss
  • If the employee violated workplace safety rules causing harm to others
  • If the employee was absent without valid reason for 20+ days in a year (or 7 consecutive days)
  • If the employee divulged company trade secrets causing harm
  • If the employee was found intoxicated or under the influence of drugs
  • If the employee physically assaulted the employer or colleagues

Employers may also deduct outstanding loan amounts from gratuity with prior agreement. However, deductions cannot reduce gratuity below the minimum legal entitlement without specific justification.

Free Zone Employees & Gratuity

Most UAE free zones follow the federal UAE Labour Law for gratuity purposes. However, two major exceptions exist:

  • DIFC (Dubai International Financial Centre): Operates under the DIFC Employment Law 2019. Gratuity is capped at 1 day per month of basic salary, and employees can opt into the DIFC Employee Workplace Savings (DEWS) scheme instead.
  • ADGM (Abu Dhabi Global Market): Follows the ADGM Employment Regulations, which have different gratuity provisions including mandatory employer contributions to a savings scheme.

If you work in other free zones (JAFZA, TECOM, Dubai Media City, RAKEZ, etc.), the federal UAE Labour Law gratuity rules generally apply.

How to Claim Your Gratuity

When your employment ends, your gratuity should be paid within 14 days of the last working day. Here is the process:

  1. Confirm your calculation: Use our UAE Gratuity Calculator to estimate your entitlement
  2. Request payment in writing: Send a formal written request to your employer or HR department
  3. Check your bank account: Gratuity is typically processed via WPS (Wage Protection System)
  4. If disputed or delayed: File a complaint with MOHRE at 800 60 or through their app
  5. Legal action: If MOHRE mediation fails, the case goes to UAE Labour Courts

Key FAQs

Is gratuity calculated on basic salary or gross salary?
Basic salary only. Do not include housing allowance, transport allowance, or any other benefits. However, if your contract states that certain allowances are part of your "basic wage", they may be included. Check your employment contract carefully.
Does unpaid leave affect my gratuity?
Yes. Authorized or unauthorized unpaid leave days are typically deducted from your total service period for gratuity calculation purposes. Get confirmation of the exact number of unpaid leave days from your employer or HR department.
Can my employer make me sign away my gratuity rights?
No. UAE Labour Law explicitly prohibits any contractual term that waives or reduces an employee's gratuity rights below the legal minimum. Any such clause in your employment contract is legally void and unenforceable.
What if my employer declares bankruptcy before paying my gratuity?
UAE law gives employees priority over most other creditors in insolvency proceedings for the recovery of wages and gratuity. File your claim with the court-appointed liquidator. MOHRE can also provide guidance in such situations.
